EXPERIENCE

Direction of arrival estimation with nonuniform sparse arrays (Professor: Lee C Potter)

  • Experimentally measure the locations of microphones, sampled sound at each location, elevation angles between the signal and microphone
  • Implement and demonstrate sparse linear and planar arrays designed with constraints and RMSE performance
  • Compute noise power, CRLB theoretical bound on error variance, MLE estimator of DOA, and PRoM estimator of DOA
  • Implement the optimized designs to determine the position that could generate the set of angle without aliasing and demonstrate its performance

Control and Intelligent Transportations Research Lab (Research scientist: Ekim Yurtsever)

  • Proposed and developed a small-scale research platform for intelligent transportation systems
  • Solved various dependencies issues and used OpenPCDet to train the Kitti Datasets
  • Propose a more sensitive and accurate approach to count the vehicles with ROSBAG extraction simultaneously, instead of using the existed video files processing method
  • The designed vehicle counting program with yolo algorithm allows to extract bidirectional vehicle counts from a moving observation platform, define the counting area with georeferencing, extract Bird's Eye View (BEV) trajectories of observed vehicles
Machine Learning Application Research (Assistant Professor: Pengtao Xie)
  • A DARTS structure-based machine learning method is proposed, which can learn Spatio-temporal data end-to-end using a spatial model, and thus apply it in the simulation of the model of COVID-Net.
  • Presented a multi-level optimization framework to formulate LBT which involves three learning stages: teacher learns; teacher teaches student; teacher and student validate themselves.
  • An efficient algorithm to solve the LBT problem has been developed and applied the approach to neural architecture search on CIFAR-100, CIFAR-10, and ImageNet, where the results demonstrate the effectiveness of our method
