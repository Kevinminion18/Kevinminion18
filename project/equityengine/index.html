EquityEngine: A Pathway to Inclusive and Personalized Engineering Education

HCI Accessibility Engineering Education

The underrepresentation of Students Living with Disabilities (SLWD) in engineering fields highlights a critical educational equity gap. This gap necessitates fundamental changes within academia to not only addresses accessibility but also embraces the full spectrum of student diversity to truly close the equity gap. Current research underscores the effectiveness of personalized learning strategies, which consistently lead to improved learning outcomes and increased student engagement, diversity, and inclusion. However, our systematic literature review reveals a pronounced scarcity of AI-driven learning tools developed specifically for post-secondary STEM education. The majority of existing tools are designed primarily for K-12 students, overlooking the distinct, more advanced challenges faced by SLWD in higher education.

Research Directions

Three core pillars of our research: Accessibility, Engineering Education, and Artificial ntelligence. By intersecting these areas, we aim to create a more inclusive and engaging learning environment for college students living with disabilities in engineering programs, and more broadly, across STEM disciplines.. The overlapping region represents the synergy where all three pillars converge, driving the development of tailored, AI-driven solutions that enhance both educational outcomes and equity for SLWD.

Key Milestones

  • Phase 1: Literature Review: Identify existing research gaps, particularly in AI-driven tools for post-secondary STEM education for SLWD. This phase focuses on understanding the current landscape and pinpointing the areas most lacking in support and technology.
  • Phase 2: Mixed-Method User Studies
    • Surveys: Conduct comprehensive surveys to gather data on demographics, learning styles, environmental factors, and experiences with existing AI-powered tools among the general student body.
    • Semi-structured Interviews: Dive deeper into SLWD’s learning experiences, both traditional and technologically enhanced, to grasp the potential and current effectiveness of AI-driven tools. This phase also explores DEI aspects within AI education environments.
  • Phase 3: Data analysis: Analyze the qualitative data from the semi-structured interviews and quantitative data from the survey to establish design principles.
  • Phase 4: Prototype Development: Create initial digital mockups of the AI-driven platform based on data-driven design principles and develop a functional prototype incorporating feedback with inspection-based testing.
  • Phase 5: Usability Testing and Iterative Design: Conduct extensive usability testing with SLWD to refine and adjust the platform. This iterative process ensures the tool is responsive to user needs and accessible in various learning environments.
  • Phase 6: Outcome Consolidation: Consolidate outcomes and publish findings.
